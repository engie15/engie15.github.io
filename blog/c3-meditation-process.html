Blog#7 Meditation Process

How did the 'process over product' concept affect the way you tackled the site redesign and rebuild?

I had a love hate relationship with the process over product concept. I absolutely loved the process of wireframes to build, then to design. It was great to have in mind what I wanted my site redesign to look like. Where I struggled was with the design. Being new, my skills are limited and what I wanted to create I don’t have the skill to do (yet). This is what made me spend more time on certain details I wanted to figure out how to create and being persistent as I know I can be – this took a lot of my time. To manage this, I used the time-blocking technique for each session where I was working on the redesign. This helped immensely. I found myself going back to my wireframe constantly because I would get side tracked into wanting to learn a different skill that wasn’t a part of my wireframe. It acted like my reset button.

The funny thing is, once I let go of the frustration and looked at my website with fresh eyes – I slightly adjusted my redesign vision and ‘Boom!’. Suddenly I had a burst of creativeness within the realm of skills I know and have to finally complete the design component. I had set 10 hours as my overall time block for this task and although I went over the total hours, the learning I had in those needed extra hours I’m grateful for.

What did you think about meditation before reading chapter 2 of Search Inside Yourself?

I’m a believer of Meditation, I’m currently working on building a daily meditation practice. My mind is constantly on and I have always wanted to know how can I give my mind a moment of peace. An opportunity to rest. This is what lead me to having an open mind about meditation all together.

What new things have I learnt about meditation?

Because I’ve learnt so many new things about meditation, I want to touch on the three that stood out to me most:

  1. The Four Step Plan to working with Distraction – mind blowing. Including meditation, I know of multiple ways I can actually apply this to my day to day living. #putitintopractice
  2. Increase in immunity – having the ability to heal yourself from within. Like WOW!
  3. The spirit of inquiry. Reading over this chapter this part stood out to me most. To me, the spirit of inquiry is how I have come to where I am today. I’m on the search on how to do certain things, how to learn certain skills, why do people do this and others that, why do things happen in this way – I mean I ask these questions (mostly to myself and at times to others) and if I don’t know – I seek out the answer in whatever way, shape or form. To know that meditation is a way to look in yourself and answer from within has me excited about what I am going to learn about myself. (I sound like such a geek. I love it!)

Did any of the suggested meditation techniques stand out to you?

Yes. I’m going to attempt to look at using Intention and applying the Four Step Plan to working with distraction in my daily practice. I’ve already started using mindfulness when I have physical distractions (e.g an itch). This has helped me to identify what needs seeing to now and what doesn’t. I can usually will an itch away now whereas before – I would just get to it. I’m also going to bump up to three minutes because I find now when my two minute buzzer goes off I don’t want it to end yet.
