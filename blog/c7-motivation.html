Blog#14 Motivation

How does life look through the ‘pleasure, passion and purpose’ lens?

I am fit toned and strong where I eat to safely and naturally heal my body. I train to move and strengthen my body. I have the means to work from where ever I may be, a digital nomad where all I need is my laptop and internet connection. I can travel the world at a drop of a hat. Where ever my heart desires I can go there and experience the world wholeheartedly.

I am my own boss. I run a company of my own and our people love what they do and have passion to do what is right and I enable them to be and become the best versions of themselves which ensures my business grows genuinely and authentically. I don’t have my own office. I don’t need it. I work with my team on the floor where although I wear the hat of ‘Founder/Director’ the way I run my business is by making efficient, well informed evidence-based decisions with my team and surround myself with the best minds in areas that are their strengths and bring balance and diversity to the team.

My company provides tangible, effective quality solutions to a diverse range community issues. Always building from the ground up. Looking for ways to build employment for communities in ways that draw on their skills. Based in NZ, Ideally, I want to begin working with Maori to create an inclusive future where we can build a path to live to our full potential and are not divided by our differences but we are accepting of them.

I am financially strong. I earn a strong solid passive income that means I no longer have to work although I choose too on projects that have meaning and heart.

Aaron and I live in our own home and care for each other. Surrounded by natural light surrounded with trees, nature and never far from the sea. Our own little Oasis. We have a state of the art efficient home. Solar powered and collects rain water. Small and compact so that we have no waste, we horde no junk and rubbish we have want we need. We have a garden full of veges and fruit. With an entertainment section of our own connected by a veranda walkway that makes it easy to walk between the different compact homes that can be adjusted to moved with ease so we always have space for all whanau and friends to come visit or live with us.

What are my core values?

  • Whanau come first – love and care for each other in the way that brings out our full potential.
  • Be open – to opportunities, to people, to differences and to all things.
  • Be kind to all beings – there are no exceptions or exclusions. Regardless of what it might have done to me before, ALWAYS treat beings with love and kindness.
  • Live by example and with integrity.
  • Be genuinely forgiving.
  • Rather than one day… what can I do today that will make my dreams a reality?
  • No is a ‘maybe’ masked in potential.
  • Words have power – be honest, open and transparent when using them.
  • Be true to myself, know myself, trust and believe in myself – what others think and believe matter to them and them only.

What do I stand strong for?

I stand strong for the right to live a life that I choose. I stand strong for equal opportunities for all no matter your background, your race, or financial status. I believe in creating equal opportunities to enable all the opportunity to live the life they choose. I stand for creating my own financial freedom in a way that enables my community – rather than in competition.

How did I find the resilience ideas and meditation exercise?

I absolutely love the resilience ideas but really need the practice executing them. I reflected a lot over the ideas and about how different I would have been if I had this training back when I was playing competitive national and international sport. I know I’ll need to practicing this especially as I head into our intensive part of our study. My meditation practice has fallen off the bandwagon a bit. With the move to NZ I had many other things I needed the time to work through and was exhausted the rest of the time. Small steps – reset and start again.
